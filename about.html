About Toad Town Services

Your Northern Wisconsin Partner for Technology Solutions

Who We Are

Toad Town Services is a one-person technology services business serving Rhinelander and Oneida County, WI. I'm Nick, the owner and the person who shows up at your door.

Background

My career has taken a lot of turns, and most of them involved figuring out how things work and fixing them when they don't.

I spent two years as Director of Operations and Technology at WXPR Public Radio, where I managed broadcast equipment, migrated the station's network infrastructure, and held FCC Chief Operator responsibility. I'm a Society of Broadcast Engineers Certified Radio Operator and hold a broadcast engineering certificate from the Cleveland Institute of Electronics.

Before and after that, I worked in IT and data analytics — building reporting systems for healthcare organizations, managing networks and compliance, deploying and supporting business technology. I've also done hands-on work across manufacturing, marine repair, broadband installation, and construction.

I have a BA in Music and Management Studies from St. Olaf College, where I spent four years as a recording engineer for the college's concert series.

Why Toad Town Services

There's no shortage of tech support options, but most are either big-box impersonal or narrowly specialized. I started Toad Town Services because I kept running into people — neighbors, community members, small organizations — who needed someone who could actually sit down with them, understand their situation, and solve the problem in front of them rather than hand it off or upsell them.

I currently volunteer with my church's audio and streaming setup, WXPR's music library and am working through my own home renovation projects. That's not filler — it means I'm actively working on the same kinds of problems my clients bring to me.

What to Expect

Clear communication about what I'm doing and what it will cost. No unnecessary upsells. If I don't know or can't help with something, I'll tell you that too.
